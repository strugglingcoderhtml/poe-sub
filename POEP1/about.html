Our Roots

Rooted 4 U Hair Studio emerges within a complex social and economic context where many people of colour feel pressured to alter their natural hair texture. Research suggests that a significant proportion of Black women continue to use chemical relaxers, often due to social expectations, lack of education about natural hair care, and convenience.

In South Africa, this trend is even more pronounced, with estimates indicating that around 75% of women choose to relax their hair, largely because relaxed hair is perceived as easier to manage. However, misuse of these chemicals can lead to scalp irritation, breakage, and long-term hair damage.

Another key factor driving the reliance on relaxers is the limited knowledge about proper natural hair care practices. Natural Afro-textured hair often requires specialised maintenance routines, yet many consumers lack access to professional education.

Cost also plays a critical role. Hair services in South Africa can range from R100 to over R1,500 for basic treatments and protective styles, making consistent professional care unaffordable for many students.

Rooted 4 U Hair Studio aims to address these intersecting challenges by offering affordable, education-driven services that prioritise hair health and empowerment.
